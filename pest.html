Pest Control

Sugarcane farming fights pests with integrated pest management (IPM), using methods like crop rotation,
natural predators, and cautious pesticide use to safeguard crops and sustainability.
This aims to sustain healthy sugarcane yields and the industry's long-term viability.

Biological Control:

  • Introduce natural enemies of pests, like parasitic wasps or predators, to keep pest populations in check. For instance, certain wasp species can prey on sugarcane borers, reducing their numbers without harming the crop..

Resistant Varieties:

  • Cultivate and use sugarcane varieties that are naturally resistant or tolerant to prevalent pests in the region. This can decrease the susceptibility of the crop to pest attacks.

Cultural Practices:

  • Implement practices like timely planting, proper drainage, and maintaining optimal plant nutrition to enhance sugarcane vigor, making it less susceptible to pest damage.

Use of Pheromones and Traps:

  • Deploy pheromones to disrupt pest mating patterns or use traps to capture and reduce pest populations without resorting to chemical control.

Judicious Pesticide Use:

  • When necessary, use pesticides selectively and according to recommended guidelines. Employing pesticides only when pest populations exceed economic thresholds can prevent unnecessary harm to beneficial organisms and the environment.
